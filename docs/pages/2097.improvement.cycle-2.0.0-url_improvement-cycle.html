The selfdriven Improvement Cycle

The Improvement Cycle is a methodology that promotes continuous incremental improvement. It is a key component of the selfdriven Operating System and includes a process of learner critical reflection. The Improvement Cycle can be utilized within the context of project/task/action management, enabling project-based learning (PBL).

This process can also be conceptualized in various ways, such as:

  • Darwinism or "Survival of the fittest", implying how best to fit into an ecosystem.
  • Playing the "Infinite" game.
  • Creating healthy or least-harmful ecosystems.

The Improvement Cycle is structured around several key outcomes:

  1. Listen & Observe: Understanding the who and why of a situation, focusing on collaboration and communication.
  2. Interpret: Transforming the why into what and how, leveraging creativity in the process.
  3. Implement: Execution phase, emphasizing collaboration and communication.
  4. Review: Reflecting back on the why, what, and how of the improvement, with a focus on reflection and communication.

The final two steps of the cycle are "Fitness" and "Reflection". The "Fitness" stage asks how fit for purpose the improvement outcome is, encouraging consideration of the pluses, minuses, and observations. The "Reflection" stage prompts consideration of one's personal impact on the outcome, emphasizing critical reflection.
